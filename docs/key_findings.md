# Key Findings

## Executive Summary

This project analyzed a simulated healthcare revenue cycle database using Microsoft SQL Server to evaluate financial performance, patient activity, provider productivity, payer performance, and operational efficiency.

The analyses demonstrate how SQL can be used to support healthcare financial reporting and revenue cycle decision-making.

---

# Major Findings

## 1. Database Overview

- Calculated the number of healthcare billing transactions.
- Identified the total number of unique patients.
- Summarized CPT code utilization by CPT grouping.

**Business Value**

Provides a high-level understanding of database size, patient population, and procedural diversity.

---

## 2. Patient & Provider Analysis

- Identified providers submitting Medicare claims.
- Measured provider participation by payer.
- Compared physician and patient volumes across healthcare locations.

**Business Value**

Supports workforce planning and payer relationship management.

---

## 3. CPT Utilization

- Identified high-volume CPT codes.
- Evaluated procedure utilization by diagnosis group.
- Analyzed J-code procedure activity.

**Business Value**

Supports coding audits, reimbursement optimization, and clinical utilization analysis.

---

## 4. Revenue Cycle Performance

- Calculated Gross Collection Rate by healthcare location.
- Compared payer payment performance.
- Identified locations with stronger financial outcomes.

**Business Value**

Provides insight into billing effectiveness and revenue collection performance.

---

## 5. Provider Specialty Analysis

- Compared physician specialties based on total payments.
- Evaluated monthly payment trends.
- Measured financial performance by specialty.

**Business Value**

Supports provider productivity analysis and financial planning.

---

## 6. Credentialing Adjustments

- Quantified revenue lost because of credentialing issues.
- Identified affected healthcare locations.
- Identified physicians associated with credentialing write-offs.

**Business Value**

Highlights opportunities to improve credentialing processes and reduce avoidable revenue loss.

---

## 7. Patient Demographics

- Generated patient demographic reports.
- Categorized patients into age groups.
- Reported geographic distributions.

**Business Value**

Supports patient population analysis and operational reporting.

---

## 8. Diabetes Patient Analysis

- Evaluated average patient age by gender.
- Identified patients diagnosed with Type 2 diabetes.
- Reported diabetes patient volumes at Big Heart Community Hospital.

**Business Value**

Demonstrates the ability to analyze disease-specific patient populations.

---

## 9. Charge & Payment Analysis

- Calculated charge per CPT unit.
- Calculated payment per CPT unit.
- Compared reimbursement across insurance payers.

**Business Value**

Supports reimbursement analysis and payer contract evaluation.

---

## 10. Net Collection Rate

- Calculated Net Collection Rate.
- Measured Accounts Receivable percentages.
- Evaluated write-off percentages.
- Compared financial performance by physician specialty.

**Business Value**

Provides insight into overall revenue cycle effectiveness and financial health.

This project demonstrates practical SQL skills applied to healthcare revenue cycle data.

The analyses illustrate how healthcare organizations can use SQL to monitor operational performance, evaluate financial outcomes, improve billing efficiency, and support data-driven decision-making.

The project also demonstrates competencies relevant to Healthcare Analytics, Revenue Cycle Analytics, Clinical Data Management, and Healthcare Business Intelligence roles.
